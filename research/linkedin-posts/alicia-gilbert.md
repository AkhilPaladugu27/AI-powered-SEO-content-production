**Full Text:**

To be mentioned is helpful. To be recommended is divine. 🕊️ 🎓

And to be recommended FIRST = a 389% higher chance of being searched on Google.

That's according to a new study from Scrunch, which looked at how AI brand recommendations across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude influence buyer behavior, specifically whether people go on to search the brand, visit its site, or view its products within the following week.

The big finding: where your brand lands in an AI answer changes everything.

📍 Named FIRST → 389% more likely to be searched
📍 Named MID-answer → 148% more likely
📍 Named LAST → 50% more likely

Answers with "best" or "top" framing boost those odds even further.

So our job is simple to say, harder to do: help our clients be the FIRST recommendation.

We're never going to be #1 for everyone. But we can be #1 for the right someone, our target audience. And that starts with knowing your ICP and your brand attributes cold.

Here's what this looks like in practice:

I hop into ChatGPT and type, "I'm looking for the best high-protein meal service under $12 per serving that delivers to Fort Myers, FL." I've never used a meal service before. I'm going with that first brand. Done.

THAT is prime real estate.

So our job becomes:
→ Look at the sources AI is citing for that kind of response
→ Find the (gasp!) listicle it's pulling from
→ Figure out how to get on it
→ Optimize for the specific attributes that matter (high protein, price point, delivery area)
→ Ensure our content across the web SCREAMS "affordable, high-protein meal service."

The takeaway. Go audit the prompts you're tracking in your AI visibility tools. Are they mostly the broad, obvious ones? Or the specific, high-intent ones your buyer is actually asking?
